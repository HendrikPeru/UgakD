David Wallace

"Suck it!"

David Wallace is a recurring character in the television series "The Office." He serves as the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) of Dunder Mifflin, the paper company where the show is set. Wallace is known for his composed and professional demeanor. Despite being the financial head of the company, he often finds himself bewildered by the eccentricities of the branch managers, particularly Michael Scott. Throughout the series, David Wallace plays a crucial role in various storylines, including company restructuring and financial troubles. Despite his authority, he becomes somewhat sympathetic to the challenges faced by the Scranton branch employees.

Jo Bennet

"You don't get to be the most powerful woman in Tallahassee by slacking off. You get there by working hard or marrying rich, and I did both"

Jo Bennett is the CEO of Sabre, the company that acquires Dunder Mifflin. She is a strong, no-nonsense leader with a Southern charm. Jo brings a different dynamic to the office and introduces several changes during her tenure.

Robert California

"Do I look like someone who would waste my own time?"

Robert California is a mysterious and eccentric character who takes over as the CEO of Dunder Mifflin after Jo Bennett. He is known for his philosophical musings, unpredictable behavior, and his ability to manipulate situations to his advantage.

Karen Filippelli

"I cried for weeks over that guy, so yeah, seeing him climb out of a PT Cruiser in a ladies warehouse uniform felt pretty good."

Karen Filippelli is a former employee of the Stamford branch who later transfers to the Scranton branch. She becomes romantically involved with Jim Halpert but faces challenges when Jim's feelings for Pam resurface.

Holly Flax

"Okay, so I've gone over this, and I've thought about it, and I just don't think there's any way I can write a report that doesn't end with her being terminated."

Holly Flax is the quirky and cheerful Human Resources representative from the Dunder Mifflin corporate office. She forms a romantic relationship with Michael Scott, and her departure from the Scranton branch becomes a significant storyline.

Jan Levinson

"Why is this so hard? That's what she said. Oh, my God, what am I saying?"

Jan Levinson is the Vice President of Northeast Sales at Dunder Mifflin. She has a tumultuous relationship with Michael Scott and undergoes various personal and professional challenges throughout the series.

Nellie Bertram

"When you use a ridiculous font, no one thinks you have a plan."

Nellie Bertram is introduced as a corporate employee who later becomes the Special Projects Manager at Dunder Mifflin. She is known for her British accent, assertive personality, and her journey from antagonist to a more sympathetic character.

Gabe Lewis

"I don't have the lung capacity to blow a whistle."

Gabe Lewis is initially the Director of Emerging Regions at Dunder Mifflin before becoming the Director of Sabre Retail. He is quirky and socially awkward, often trying to fit in with his colleagues, particularly Erin Hannon.

DeAngelo Vickers

"Yeah I got tons of time. This job's a joke."

DeAngelo Vickers is briefly the regional manager at the Scranton branch. He is known for his eccentric personality, passion for magic, and his short-lived and tumultuous tenure as the office manager.

Mose Schrute

Honorable mention for his impecable parking skills, his fear of Harry Potter and his love for Lady Scarecrow.

Mose Schrute is Dwight Schrute's eccentric cousin and farmhand. Mose rarely speaks, but his peculiar and reclusive behavior on the beet farm adds a unique and humorous element to the show.
